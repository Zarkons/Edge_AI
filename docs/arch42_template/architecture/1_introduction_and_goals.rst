1. Introduction And Goals
=========================

.. Functional requirements are usually derived elsewhere, the architecural document should reference them in order to satisfy them and provide traceability matrix. So this section provides the list of functional requirements.

.. Additionally to functional requirements, quality goals need to be defined. Quality goals can be derived from the functionality the system provides (related to performance, reliability, etc.), from the business context (related to cost, time to market, etc.) or from the technical context (related to maintainability, portability, etc.). Quality goals can also be derived from the processes, standards, and regulations that the system needs to comply with.
.. This section provides the list of quality goals.

.. Functional requirements describe the behavior of the system, while quality goals drive the architecture!

.. This section also provides a list of relevant stakeholders and their expectations. Stakeholders can be internal (e.g., developers, testers, managers) or external (e.g., customers, regulators, partners). The expectations of stakeholders can be related to functional requirements, quality goals, or other aspects of the system.
